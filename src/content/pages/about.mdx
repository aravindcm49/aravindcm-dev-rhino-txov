## Intro

I'm aravindcm — a developer interested in how software systems age, how ideas propagate through codebases, and what makes tools worth keeping around for more than a few months.

This site is a curated space for writing, references, and projects that I think are worth returning to. It's not a blog in the traditional sense — more like a shelf I maintain over time.

## Work and Interests

I spend most of my time thinking about:

- **Software longevity** — why some systems outlive their creators and others don't
- **Developer tooling** — the gap between what we build and what we actually enjoy using
- **Personal knowledge systems** — how to accumulate insight without accumulating clutter
- **Web architecture** — static-first, progressive enhancement, and the value of simplicity

I'm drawn to projects that solve a real problem well, rather than projects that solve many problems adequately.

## Principles

A few things I try to keep in mind:

- **Simplicity over cleverness.** If I can't explain it to someone in a few sentences, it's probably too complex.
- **Curate, don't accumulate.** The value of a collection is what you choose to leave out.
- **Build for longevity.** Software that works in ten years is better than software that's impressive today.
- **Own your platform.** A personal site is a place where the rules are yours.

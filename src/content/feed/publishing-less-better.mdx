Some writers treat a personal site like a journal: every thought, every link, every day. That produces
volume, but volume is not the point. A personal site is more like a shelf you maintain over years —
what earns a place on the shelf is what you are willing to still stand behind a year from now.

The cost of publishing anything is the cost of maintaining it. A dead link, a stale argument, an old
tool — these compound. The things that earn permanence earn it by being rare.
